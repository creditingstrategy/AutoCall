AutoCall Crediting

Growth when the market cooperates. A second chance when it doesn’t.

A down year doesn’t have to mean a lost coupon.

Each anniversary asks one question: Is the index at or above the current strike price? If yes, a coupon is paid, along with any coupons previously missed, and the strike price resets higher. If no, no coupon is paid that year, but it is not lost – it goes into memory and can be paid if the index later recovers.

Key definitions

Strike price

The index level that must be met or exceeded on each annual anniversary date for a coupon to be paid. When a coupon is paid, the strike price resets to the index level at the close on that anniversary date.

Coupon

The annual interest payment earned when the index has a qualifying anniversary.

How it works

One annual check, two outcomes for growth: receive a coupon payment, or put it into storage.

Each anniversary compares the index to the current strike price. If the anniversary qualifies, the annual coupon is paid immediately. If it does not, the coupon is stored in memory and may be earned back in a future year if the index recovers.

1

Check the anniversary level

On each anniversary, the index is measured against the strike price – the level established at the start of the contract or reset at the most recent qualifying anniversary.

2

If the strike price is met

The annual coupon is paid along with any coupons held in memory, and the strike price resets upward to its new level.

3

If the strike price is not met

No coupon is paid for that year and the coupon is stored in memory. The strike price remains unchanged until a future anniversary qualifies.

Memory feature

Missed does not mean gone.

When the index finishes below the strike price on an annual anniversary date, that year’s coupon is not lost. Instead, it is stored in memory and carried forward to a future qualifying anniversary.

If, on a later anniversary date, the index closes at or above the current strike price, the current year’s coupon will be paid together with any coupons stored in memory. This is what creates a true second chance after a non-qualifying year.

Illustrative memory release
Annual Coupon: 9%

In this example, the index declined over the first two years and finished below its strike price in Years 1 and 2, so no coupons were paid and 9% per year was stored in memory. When the index recovered above its strike price in Year 3, a total of 27% was credited — 9% for the current year plus 18% from memory. The strike price then reset to this new level. Year 4 was negative, so another 9% went into memory. When the index recovered in Year 5 and finished above its new strike price, coupons from both years were credited for a total of 18%.

FAQs

Common questions

What happens if the index finishes below the strike price this year?

If the index finishes below the strike price on the anniversary date, no coupon is paid for that year. Instead, the coupon is banked in memory and may be paid later if a future anniversary qualifies.

What happens to the strike price after a qualifying anniversary?

When an anniversary qualifies, the strike price resets upward to the index level recorded on that anniversary date.

Will the size of the annual coupon change every year?

No. The annual coupon is locked in for the term of your annuity.

Does the memory feature only work for one missed year?

No. The memory feature is not limited to a single missed year. If the index does not qualify for multiple anniversaries, the missed coupons can continue to accumulate in memory and may be paid when the index later recovers, and a future anniversary qualifies.
